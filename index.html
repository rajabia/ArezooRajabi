About Me

I am working as a postdoctoral scholar at Network Security Lab, University of Washington. I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from Oregon State University (OSU) under supervision of Prof. Rakesh B. Bobba and my Master’s and Bachelor’s degrees in Computer Engineeing and Computer Science at Sharif University of Technology. My Ph.D. project focused on Image Privacy and intersection of Machine Learning and Cybersecurity. I also worked as gradurate research assistant at Digital Media Lab and conducted research on complex networks analysis methods and graph theories.

My Research Interests

  • Robustness in Deep Neural Networks: Exploring the vulnerabilities of deep neural networks and developing defenses to mitigate them

  • Differential Privacy: developing differential privacy solutions and defenses against membership inference attacks

  • Cyber-Security: developing fault tolerance algorithms in distributed learning methods
